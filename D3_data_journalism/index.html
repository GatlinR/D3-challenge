Correlations of Smokers across States

Findings on How Age and Income Affect Likelihood of Smoking

The median age of citizens living in a state is listed in correlation to the percentage of smokers in that state. The younger a state's population is seems to infer less smokers residing there.

Annual Household Income appears to be an excellent indicator of identifying smokers. States with low household incomes have a higher percentage of smokers. States with higher household incomes tend to be less likely to smoke.

Comparing those living in poverty also reinforces the assumption above that infers that those who bring in less income are more likely to be smokers. The higher a state has citizens living in poverty, the more smokers they have residing in their state.
